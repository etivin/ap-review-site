Writing Practice

SAQ, DBQ & LEQ

Write a response and get feedback from AI using real AP rubric criteria.

SAQ Prompt — Use the source to answer A, B, and C“The peace conditions imposed upon Germany are so hard, so humiliating. The financial burden is so heavy that it is no exaggeration to say that Germany is reduced to economic bondage. Chained and enslaved, Germany will always remain a menace to Europe.” — Algemeen Handelsblad, Dutch newspaper, June 1919

A. Identify ONE specific argument the author makes about the Treaty of Versailles.
B. Explain ONE way the Treaty of Versailles contributed to political instability in Germany during the interwar period.
C. Explain ONE way a nation other than Germany responded to the political or economic instability of the interwar period.
SAQ Tips from Mr. TAnswer ONLY what is asked. Identify = 1–2 sentences. Explain = 2–3 sentences with a “because” or “this led to.” Do NOT write full paragraphs — it is unnecessary and costs you time.
DBQ PromptEvaluate the extent to which government propaganda and mobilization shaped the conduct of World War I and World War II in the period 1914–1945.

Write a full DBQ response: thesis with line of reasoning, contextualization (2–3 sentences), evidence from at least 4 documents (use the Watson, Goldfarb, and Soviet poster stimuli from your MCQ section as your documents), and one piece of outside evidence. HIPP at least 2 documents.
DBQ Formula from Mr. T¶1: Thesis (adverb + “as seen through” two groups) + Contextualization. ¶2: Group 1 — summarize 2 docs + explain + HIPP one doc + outside evidence. ¶3: Group 2 — same structure. Use all 7 docs for complexity point.
LEQ PromptEvaluate the extent to which the consequences of World War I caused World War II in the period 1919–1939.
LEQ Formula from Mr. TNo documents — all from memory. ¶1: Thesis (adverb + “as seen through”) + Contextualization. ¶2: Evidence 1 (describe in detail + explain why it supports your argument). ¶3: Evidence 2. Frame around causation, CCOT, or comparison.

Writing Tips

Mr. T’s FRQ Playbook

Everything you need to write the SAQ, DBQ, and LEQ on exam day.

📝 SAQ — Short Answer

Answer ONLY what is asked. No intros, no conclusions.

  • Identify = 1–2 sentences
  • Describe = 2 sentences
  • Explain = 2–3 sentences with “because”

You must answer Q1 and Q2. Choose between Q3 or Q4 for the last one.

📄 Thesis Formula

Works for both DBQ and LEQ. Must be historically defensible with a line of reasoning.

“The [subject] [adverb] affected [object] as seen through [group 1] and [group 2].”

Adverb evaluates extent: significantly, fundamentally, minimally, primarily, negatively…

🕐 Contextualization

A historical process that occurred before/during the prompt period that led to the topic.

  • Don’t start at “the dawn of man”
  • Stay within ~50 years of the prompt
  • Must be 2–3 full sentences — not a phrase
  • Explain HOW it connects to the prompt

📄 DBQ Evidence

Three evidence points:

  • Points 1–2: Accurately use 4+ of 7 docs. “Document X shows [idea]. This supports my argument because…”
  • Point 3: Outside evidence — a relevant fact NOT in the documents, same time frame

🔍 HIPP — Sourcing

HIPP at least 2 documents. Pick ONE element per doc and explain its significance.

H

Historical Situation — context when written

I

Intended Audience — who it was written for

P

Point of View — who wrote it and why

P

Purpose — what the author was trying to do

End with: “This alters/supports my understanding because…”

📝 LEQ — Long Essay

No documents — everything from memory. Frame around one reasoning skill.

  • Causation: What caused / what resulted
  • CCOT: What changed and what stayed the same
  • Comparison: Similarities and differences

“One piece of evidence is [specific detail]. This supports my argument because…”

Complexity Point Options
✦ Explain multiple causes/effects, similarities/differences, or continuities/changes
✦ Explain both cause AND effect, or both similarity AND difference
✦ Connect across time periods or geographic areas
✦ Use all 7 documents effectively (DBQ) or HIPP 4+ documents
A counterargument paragraph can earn complexity — but only if it is a full paragraph with evidence, not just a sentence.
